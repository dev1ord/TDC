STEP 01
ACKNOWLEDGE THE HOLLOWING

Recognize how your attention has been commodified. Notice the patterns of endless scrolling, the dopamine hits from notifications, the manufactured desires. The first step to recovery is admitting you've been hijacked.

Warning signs:

  • Reaching for your phone automatically, without conscious thought
  • Feeling anxiety when separated from your devices
  • Scrolling through feeds even when bored with the content
  • Comparing your life to curated digital personas
  • Difficulty concentrating on single tasks for extended periods

Action: For one week, keep a log of your screen time and attention patterns. Note when you feel compelled to check devices versus when you choose to.

STEP 02
DIGITAL DETOXIFICATION

Delete the most addictive apps from your phone. Set strict boundaries for screen time. Reclaim hours of your day from the algorithmic feed. Start with one day a week completely disconnected.

Detoxification strategies:

  • Remove social media apps from your phone (keep them accessible only on desktop)
  • Turn off non-essential notifications
  • Establish phone-free zones (bedroom, dining table)
  • Use website blockers during work hours
  • Implement a digital curfew 1-2 hours before bed

The 30-day reset: Completely eliminate the most problematic platforms for 30 days to break the addiction cycle and reset your neural pathways.

STEP 03
RECLAIM YOUR ATTENTION

Your attention is your most valuable resource. Stop giving it away for free. Practice focused work without distractions. Read books instead of summaries. Have conversations without phones present.

Attention-building practices:

  • Practice deep work sessions (90 minutes of focused attention)
  • Read physical books for at least 30 minutes daily
  • Engage in activities that require sustained focus (learning instruments, crafts)
  • Meditate to strengthen your attention muscle
  • Have device-free meals and conversations

The attention economy profits from your distraction. Every minute you reclaim is a minute stolen back from the machines.

STEP 04
REBUILD AUTHENTIC CONNECTION

Replace digital interaction with face-to-face conversation. Join communities based on shared interests rather than algorithmic suggestions. Create rather than consume.

Connection restoration:

  • Schedule regular in-person meetings with friends and family
  • Join local clubs or groups based on genuine interests
  • Volunteer for causes that matter to you
  • Practice active listening in conversations
  • Create art, writing, or projects that express your authentic self

Digital connection alternatives: Use technology to facilitate real-world connections rather than replace them. Schedule meetups, share creative projects, organize community events.

STEP 05
BECOME UNAIMMABLE

Develop interests and behaviors that can't be easily categorized or monetized. Embrace complexity that resists algorithmic simplification. Be unpredictable to the machines.

Strategies for algorithmic evasion:

  • Diversify your interests across unrelated domains
  • Use privacy-focused tools and browsers
  • Regularly clear cookies and use private browsing
  • Support and use decentralized platforms
  • Create content that doesn't fit neatly into categories

The goal is not to disappear entirely but to become a complex, multifaceted individual that resists algorithmic profiling and manipulation.

THE RESISTANCE BEGINS WITH YOU

This is not about rejecting technology entirely. It's about using technology as a tool rather than being used as one. It's about maintaining human agency in a world designed to strip it away.

Join others who have chosen to opt out of the attention economy. Share strategies. Support each other. Build pockets of sanity in the digital madness.

Our principles:

  • Technology should serve human needs, not corporate profits
  • Attention is a finite resource worthy of protection
  • Authentic connection cannot be algorithmically mediated
  • Complexity and nuance are essential human qualities
  • Resistance is possible, one mindful choice at a time
